How to Insert Audio Files in PDF

  • by Shero
  • July 5, 2023
  • PDF Embedding, PDF Tips & Tools

Adding audio to your PDFs can bring a new level of interactivity and engagement, which allows you to create captivating multimedia experiences for your readers. In this article, we will guide you on how to insert audio files to PDF. We’ll cover the manual method using the popular PDF editing software – Adobe Acrobat, and an online solution for easily embedding audio in PDF.

Method1: Insert Audio in PDF Using Adobe Acrobat

Adobe Acrobat provides a comprehensive set of tools for creating and modifying PDF documents. Follow these step-by-step instructions to embed audio files into your PDF using Adobe Acrobat:

  1. Launch Adobe Acrobat: Open Adobe Acrobat on your computer and open the PDF document you want to add audio to.
  2. Prepare Audio Files: Make sure your audio files are in a compatible format such as MP3 or WAV. If necessary, convert your audio files to the desired format using audio editing software.
  3. Access Multimedia Tools: In Adobe Acrobat, go to the top menu and select “Tools” > “Rich Media”.
  4. Choose Audio Tool: From the Rich Media panel, select the “Add Video or Sound” tool.
  5. Place Audio Annotation: Click and drag on the page where you want to add the audio annotation. This will create a placeholder for your audio file.
  6. Set Audio Properties: In the options panel, click “Open” to select the audio file you want to embed. Adjust the display settings, such as appearance and playback controls, based on your preferences.
  7. Configure Playback Options: Customize the audio settings, including autoplay, loop, and volume, using the options available in the properties panel.
  8. Save Changes: Once you have configured the desired settings, save the changes to your PDF document.
  9. Test Audio Playback: Open your PDF in a PDF viewer or Adobe Acrobat to test the audio playback. Ensure that the audio plays as intended and adjust any settings if needed.

Method2: Insert Audio in PDF Using FlipHTML5 (Online)

Although Adobe Acrobat Pro DC is a powerful tool, it’s too hefty and complex for people with few design or computer skills. Here is where FlipHTML5 comes in handy. FlipHTML5 offers a dummy-proof online PDF editor for users to embed video in PDF, add audio, gifs, image galleries, hyperlinks to text, images or even buttons into the PDF.

  1. Upload Your PDF

    Go to the FlipHTML5 website and sign in to your account. If you don’t have an account, create one for free. Once logged in, upload the PDF document to the FlipHTML5 platform by clicking on the “Create New Flipbook” button and selecting your PDF file from your computer.

  2. Add “Audio”

    After uploading, you’ll enter the edit mode. In the toolbar, click on the “Edit Pages” button to access the page editing options. Choose the page where you want to add audio and click on the “Insert Audio” icon in the toolbar. Select the audio file you want to add from your computer and upload it to the FlipHTML5 platform.

  3. Configure Playback Settings

    Customize the playback settings for your audio file, such as autoplay, loop, and controls visibility, according to your preferences.

  4. Preview and share

    Once you have finished configuring the audio settings, save the changes to your flipbook and preview to see if your audio file is all set on your flipbook. When it’s ready, grab the generated book link to share.

That’s it! You have successfully added audio in PDF through FlipHTML5.
